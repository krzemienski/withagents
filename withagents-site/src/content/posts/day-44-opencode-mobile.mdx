Four lines of JSON in `~/.config/opencode-mobile/tunnel-config.json`:

```json
{
  "provider": "cloudflare",
  "mode": "free",
  "cloudflaredPath": "/opt/homebrew/bin/cloudflared"
}
```

That was the whole mobile patch. Not an iOS app. Not a rewrite. A tunnel contract between my phone and the laptop running `opencode`, sitting in a dotfile most people will never see.

I spent three sessions trying to do more than that. Each one failed the same way.

## What opencode is, and what it is not

opencode is an open-source coding agent. It is not my code. The upstream project lives under `opencode-ai` on npm and ships a CLI that talks to whatever model you route it at. I have been running it against Claude for about a month, mostly as a second opinion when my primary stack got stuck.

"opencode-mobile" is not a fork. There is no separate repo. It is a pattern, a config directory under `~/.config/opencode-mobile/` and a cloudflared tunnel, that lets the desktop instance answer prompts I type from my phone.

The reason it matters for this series is the mode-bet. opencode is the canonical SDK bet: you write code (and in this case, config) to steer the agent. Everything interesting about the mobile story is what happens when an SDK-mode product meets a consumer-mode surface.

## The three failed attempts

**Attempt one: native iOS client.** I sketched a SwiftUI app that would embed the opencode runtime via a bridge. I got as far as the keystore before I admitted I was rebuilding `claude-mobile-expo`, which already exists and is the Day 45 story. Delete.

**Attempt two: reverse-tunnel via ngrok.** Worked. Cost $8/month. I was paying for the privilege of exposing a process that already runs locally. The pricing sheet was the feedback: if the economics don't match the product, the architecture is wrong.

**Attempt three: Tailscale.** Also worked. Required every device in my life to join a mesh, and my phone carrier doesn't love that profile. Two out of three connections dropped during actual use, which in mobile-agent work means the whole interaction aborts mid-thought.

Cloudflared free tier, with one line pointing at the homebrew binary, survived all three scenarios I had used to kill the others. I stopped engineering.

## What the four lines actually bought

The tunnel config is the entire API surface. Everything else is standard:

- `opencode` runs on the laptop with its normal config. No mobile-specific fork.
- `cloudflared` opens a named tunnel, pointed at the local HTTP port opencode already exposes.
- The phone hits the tunnel URL through Safari (or a bare shortcut). No custom client.
- Auth is cloudflared's access policy plus the local opencode's token check. Two layers, neither of them mine to maintain.

The lesson the three failed attempts taught me is that in SDK-mode products, the "mobile version" is almost always the wrong framing. The product is already on the laptop. What you need is a legible boundary, not a second implementation.

## Where the mode-bet ends

opencode is the clearest SDK-mode bet I run. You configure it in code, you invoke it from a shell, you pipe it around like any other CLI. That discipline is what makes it good. It is also what makes "iOS-native opencode" nonsense.

The mobile story for an SDK product is a tunnel and an auth policy. The mobile story for an Interactive product (skills, hooks, a rich environment) is a different thing, and that is where `claude-mobile-expo` actually earns a separate binary. The mobile story for a Non-Interactive product (headless loops) is different again, and that is where Ralph-on-iOS lives.

Three products, three mobile stories, one rule: build the surface the runtime actually needs, not the surface the App Store expects.

## The thing I still do not have

I do not have secure multi-device sync. If I move from the phone to a tablet mid-session, opencode has no idea the caller changed. The tunnel sees one origin. The laptop sees one client. The session is the same, but there is no identity continuity.

I have been pretending that is a feature ("the laptop is the identity, not the device"), and some of the time it is. Some of the time it is exactly the gap that makes me open a terminal instead of the phone, which defeats the point of the tunnel.

If you have solved this without a full identity provider in the loop, I would like to know how. I do not have a great answer yet.

## What ships, what doesn't

What ships on Day 44 of this push: the tunnel config pattern, documented. Four lines of JSON, one tunnel, one cloudflared binary path. That is the whole footprint.

What does not ship: a native client, a custom auth layer, a sync protocol. Those are each their own post, and only one of them (`claude-mobile-expo`) earns its binary.

The full mobile surface is going to take more than a tunnel. But on the laptop where the SDK bet lives, the tunnel config is the whole product.
