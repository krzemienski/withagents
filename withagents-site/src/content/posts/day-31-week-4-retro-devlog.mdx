Twenty-nine days in, the rest week starts today.

Four flagships cleared. ValidationForge GA on Day 01. CCB Evolution on Day 10. Ralph Orchestrator origin on Day 22. The skills package launch on Day 35 is still ahead, and SessionForge plus Code Stories plus the closing manifesto on Day 50 is the one I am genuinely nervous about. Three products in one day is dense and I have been arguing with myself about whether to split it since Day 24.

## What shipped

Ten light-edits against existing post-02 through post-18. Nineteen net-new posts. Every active day got a blog canonical, a LinkedIn Article, and an X thread. No LinkedIn Shorts: the channel matrix decision from R4 held. The thread count clears the $100/month X API tier by a wide margin; manual posting at this cadence would have cost me a workday per week.

## What dragged

The Ralph arc forced a correction mid-push. Product-inventory.md had `ralph-orchestrator` tagged as an external fork from an earlier mine. User correction flagged it as Nick's code. The Day 22 flagship got rewritten under time pressure. I should have caught that in the pre-push verification pass. Adding *repo-ownership confirmation for every flagship-mentioned project* to the Day-60 retro checklist.

The Shannon ecosystem expansion from one post to four was the other drag. Three of the four Shannon repos needed liveness verification before I could publish anything that pointed at them. Two of them had stale READMEs that I ended up rewriting as part of the post draft, which doubled the effort for Days 16 and 17.

## What I moved

Day 56 ILS-iOS was conditional on the pickup session landing by Day 54. It has not landed. Reserve C (phone-to-desktop bridge) is queued for the swap-in. Day 57 Remodex is blocked on the naming decision (Remodex vs Transduct, still unresolved), so Reserve C takes that slot if Day 56 did not already consume it. I expect to call this on Day 54.

## What the rest week is for

Four days off the calendar (30, 32, 33), one devlog day (31, this post). The rest week is not for catching up. The rest week is for reading comments, answering LinkedIn inbound, and staying off the drafting queue. Every push I have done where the rest week became a catch-up sprint ended the second half of the calendar worse than the first. I am going to honor the rest week this time.

Week 5 starts Day 34 with the skills track. devlog-publisher, then functional-validation, then ck-plan, then visual-explainer, then deepen-prompt-plan, then ai-dev-operating-system: six skill spotlights landing into the Day 35 package launch. The draft-to-publish ratio on the skills is better than the flagship posts, because the skills already exist and every post is a walkthrough of a real `.claude/skills/{skill}/SKILL.md` file. The writing cost is sub-1,000 words per skill because the artifact does most of the talking.
